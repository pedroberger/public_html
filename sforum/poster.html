Poster Guidelines

Poster Size

  • The poster size is A0 format (dimension of 841 mm width x 1189 cm height)

Suggestions for Formatting Your Poster

  • Place heading in large‚Äêsized type at the top of the board
  • List the title and all authors
  • All material should be readable from distances of one meter or more
  • You are strongly encouraged to use a type that is at least 22 pt. minimum
  • Use bold type to ensure legibility
  • Make all print very dark (preferably block style) on a very light (preferably white) background
  • Visuals should be simple. Leave out or remove any unnecessary details.
  • Use color to enhance comprehension, not to decorate the poster (use of color can visually enhance your poster)
  • Use letters, arrows, numbers, or other devices to show those who will be viewing your poster the preferred sequence in which your poster should be reviewed
  • Charts, drawings, and illustrations should be similar to those you would use in making power point slides
  • Present numerical data in the form of graphs, rather then tables (graphs make trends in the data much more evident). If data must be presented in table-form, KEEP IT SIMPLE
  • Make sure that any visual can "stand alone" (i. e., graph axes are properly labeled, maps have north arrows and distance scales, symbols are explained, etc.)
