25TH SYMPOSIUM ON INTEGRATED CIRCUIT AND SYSTEMS DESIGN

CHIP IN BRASILIA

August 30 - 02, 2012 - Brasilia, DF - Brazil

Call for Papers

(PDF version)

SBCCI is an international forum dedicated to integrated circuits and systems design, test and electronic design automation (EDA), held annually in Brazil. The 25th SBCCI will take place at Brasília, Brazil's capital. The goal of the symposium is to bring together researchers in the areas of EDA, design and test of integrated circuits and systems. The scope of the symposium includes technical sessions, tutorials and panels, as well as an exhibition and working group meetings. The best papers presented at the symposium will be invited to resubmit an extended version to be considered for publication at the IEEE Design & Test and at the JICS - Journal of Integrated Circuits and Systems.

SBCCI strongly encourages international submissions. Prospective authors from around the world are invited to submit original manuscripts in English for consideration by the Program Committee. Manuscripts must contain a maximum of 10 pages, using 11-point font or larger and 1.5 or double spacing and be prepared for blind review. Authors are encouraged to submit the paper in the final publication version as two-column, 6-page papers, following the ACM Press guidelines.

The submission is double blind, so please avoid any possibility to disclose the reviewer the identity of the authors.

Submissions of manuscripts previously published at other conferences or journals will not be considered for publication by the program committee. SBCCI submissions must be made electronically (in PDF format) via the conference website. Authors should include the complete address, phone/fax numbers and email address, and designate a contact person and a presenter. For proposals on special sessions, tutorials, panels and tool demos, please email a short description to the program chairs directly in advance of the submission deadline. For additional inquiries on submissions, please contact the program chairs. The proceedings will be published by the ACM Press and will remain available at the ACM Digital Library. The submission link is: https://www.softconf.com/c/SBCCI2012/

Tracks

In this edition the submissions to SBCCI will be made to one of four tracks, whichever is the most appropriate at the authors judgment. Efforts are going towards a fair balance of the published papers in equal parts in the different areas.

The tracks and their respective Track Chairs are organized as follows:

Track 1: Analog & RF & Mixed Signal

Track Chair: Carlos Galup, UFSC - Brazil

  • RF Design
  • Analog and mixed signal designs
  • Micro-electromechanical systems
  • Nanoelectronic circuits and nanoarchitectures
  • Harvesting / Scavenging Energy circuits

Track 2: CAD, Verification & Test

Track Chair: Fernando Moraes, PUCRS - Brazil

  • Physical Design
  • System and circuit synthesis
  • Design space and architectural exploration
  • Design verification and simulation
  • Testability issues and design for test techniques

Track 3: Digital, Reconfigurable & Applications

Track Chair: Diana Göhringer, Fraunhofer IOSB - DE

  • Design, specification and modeling languages and applications
  • Reconfigurable architectures and novel applications of FPGAs
  • Hardware-software co-design and co-verification
  • Emulation and prototyping techniques

Track 4: SoC, NoC, Embedded

Track Chair: Rodolfo de Azevedo, Unicamp - Brazil

  • Embedded systems design and industrial applications
  • NoC design
  • Systems-on-chip, IP reuse, MPSoC and platform-based designs
  • Low power and design techniques
